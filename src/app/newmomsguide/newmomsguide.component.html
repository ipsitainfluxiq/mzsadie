EXERCISE

Exercise Regimen for New Moms

1. Walking

Most people do not consider walking to be an exercise, but walking is actually one of the easiest ways to ease yourself back into a fitness routine after giving birth. Everything from a lovely stroll around the park to a pumped-up power walk in the mall. Anyway you can manage to get walking into your life can help start the changes you want to see in your body. Also bringing baby along in a front carrier or sling can add to the benefits of walking due to the added weight.

To switch things up, try walking backward or walking in a zigzag motion to keep your muscles guessing. It is not advised to include baby in these exercise activities until you are completely certain that you have mastered them and are sure of your balance.

2. Deep belly breathing with abdominal contraction

Deep belly breathing with abdominal contraction is a very simply and easy exercise. You can actually begin this exercise within hours after childbirth. It will start the process of strengthening and toning your abs and belly, while helping your muscles relax.

1 Sit in the upright position and breathe deeply

2 Inhaling while tightening your abs and relax while exhaling.

3 Slowly begin to increase the amount of time you can contract and hold your abs.

3. Shoulder lifts and head lifts

These two movements help tone your tummy and abs, while also burning calories.They also strengthen back muscles.

1 Start by laying on the floor or on your mat with your arms along your sides.

2 Keeping your lower back flat on the floor, bend your knees so that the bottoms of your feet touch the floor.

3 Relax your tummy as you inhale.

4 As you exhale, slowly lift your head and neck off the floor.

5 Inhale as you lower your head back down.

When you can easily do 10 head lifts then start to add shoulder lifts.

1 Get in the same position you were in for head lifts.

2 Inhale and relax your tummy.

3 As you exhale slowly, lift your head and shoulders off the floor, reaching with your arms and hands toward your bent knees. If this strains your neck, fold both hands behind your head instead.

4 Inhale as you lower your head and shoulders back down.

4. The Curl Up

After you are able to accomplish 10 shoulder lifts, next you will be able to move on to the curl up. Begin by starting out in the same position as the shoulder lift. Instead of only lifting your head and shoulders, lift your torso as well until itâ€™s about midway between your knees and the floor behind you. Extend your arms forward and reach towards your knees and remain in this position for a count of five. Then slowly lower yourself to the beginning position. Make sure to breathe while performing this exercise, as this is essential to the exercise. Exhale when you exert. Inhale when you relax.

5. Kneeling pelvic tilt

Begin by kneeling down on all fours. Make certain that your toes are touching the floor behind you, keep your arms straight down from your shoulder line, and palms touching the floor. Keep your back relaxed and straight. Do not let your back become curved or arched anytime during the exercise. While you inhale, bring your glutes forward tilt your pelvis and rotate your pubic bone upward. Stay here for a count of five, and release.

6. The Kegel

This exercise is ideal for women who have just given birth. Kegel exercises help tone and control their bladder muscles which helps reduce the risk of incontinence associated with childbirth.This exercise involves contracting and holding the muscles that control the flow of urine. To identify which muscles they are, start by doing the exercise while you use the bathroom. As you urinate, maneuver your muscles until the stream temporarily stops. Then release and let the urine flow. Remember what that feels like, and when you're not urinating, contract, hold, and release those same muscles. The more you do and the longer you hold those muscles, the better control you will have over those leaks caused by sneezing, laughing, or picking up your baby.

Bonus workouts for baby and mom

In the first couple of months it is difficult to find time away from your baby, so try some workout moves with your infant. Before attempting to try these bonus exercises, you must be certain that you have good balance and are fit enough to assure you and your baby's safety. Take some time to practice them without your baby. Do them first with nothing in your arms. Then practice while holding a doll or a rolled up blanket or towel that's the same size as your baby. Only include your baby when you're certain there's no danger of falling or dropping what you are holding in your arms.

The baby glider -- Holding your baby close to your chest across your arms, take one-step forward and bend your knee (a lunge). Return your leg and repeat with the opposite leg. This will not only help strengthen your legs but will also strengthen your back muscles and abdomen. Repeat 15 times.

The baby bouncer -- From the same position as in the baby glider, take one step out to the side (instead of to the front) and attempt to squat.

Rock-a-baby squats and curls -- Stand with your legs shoulder length apart. Holding your baby tight and close to your chest, squat down allowing your baby's feet to touch the floor. As you rise up, bring the baby back closer to your chest. Do not try this exercise until your baby is at least 10 to 12 weeks old. Repeat 15 times.

Weight Loss

Weight Loss Tips for New Moms

1. Know what your body needs

Follow these guidelines for your daily caloric intake and other nutritional needs to lose weight safely and to keep good nutrition. Calorie needs may vary depending on age, metabolism and activity level. If Youâ€™re Breastfeeding: Calories: 2,200 - 2,400 Calcium: 1,000 - 1,300 mg Folate: 280 mcg Iron: 15 mg Protein: 65g Vitamin C: 95 mg If You're Not Breastfeeding: Calories: 1,900 - 2,200 Calcium: 1,300 mg Folate: 180 mcg Iron: 15 mg Protein: 44 - 50 g Vitamin C: 60 mg These nutrients are essential if you have just given birth because they all serve a specific purpose. Folate is important, as it helps with maintaining future pregnancies. Vitamin D and calcium are vital for bone health. Iron helps with anemia. Vitamin C is needed for iron absorption. Protein is critical for building and repairing damaged muscle tissue. Try to consume more of the nutrients during the lactation process because they leave your body through breastfeeding.

2. Keep healthy food available

Making sure that you have healthy and low fat foods available will dramatically improve your chance to weight loss. We suggest stocking up on low fat and fat free yogurt; low fat deli meats; low fat or fat free pudding made with milk or containing 30 percent calcium; part skim cheese sticks; pre packaged sliced fruits and vegetables; ready- made salads; cooked whole grains such as brown rice; whole grain cereals, breads and pastas. Having these type of snacks on hand will make it easier to pick something healthy, instead of indulging on highly processed munchies like chips, cookies and cakes.

3.Donâ€™t indulge but eat often

It has been advised that consuming three small meals, plus two to three snacks per day will aid in weight loss. Try snacking on fruits, vegetables and lean protein sources between meals. Eating often while breastfeeding is important because you need calories in order to fuel milk production. It is also very important to stay properly hydrated, so be sure to

drink plenty of water. Eating smaller meals, more often throughout the day will help keep sustained energy. When you are overly hungry you are more likely to binge on sugary foods for energy.This also aids with weight loss because you feel less hungry during the day.

4.Listen to your body

Before giving in to the urge to sit down and devour your next meal, think about these few things first. Am I really hungry? If yes, you may give yourself permission to eat. If you answer no, then make sure that your eating is not a means to end boredom or emotional eating. Instead you can try to call a friend or family member, go for a stroll around the neighborhood, read a book, do something
productive. Whatever you do in order to distract your mind, do not eat because you can not think of anything better to do. What exactly am I hungry for?You can also try snacking on something healthy with a crunch. Foods that have that crunch can satisfy your appetite, so try eating a few cups of air-popped or low-fat popcorn, also an inexpensive, tasty and well worth the effort, home-baked chickpeas will deliver not just crunch, but all-important fibre, iron and folate to your diet, nuts, frozen grapes, cut up fresh vegetables, baked pita or tortilla chips, and apples and nut butter.

Although giving in to cravings often is not advised, occasionally, it is ok and actually may be helpful. If it is a craving for ice cream that you have, try having an ice cream that is low fat or a frozen yogurt. If you are constantly fighting your craving youâ€™re bound to give in and have the ice cream-after youâ€™ve already eaten the yogurt, then the nuts, then the cheese. While eating your meals try checking in on yourself to see if you are still hungry. Every 5 to 10 seconds, pause from eating to see if you are still hungry or if your stomach is full..

5. Crazy portion sizes

We have become accustomed to supersized meal portions, everything from hamburgers to sodas. Portion sizes have gotten out of control, and we have a few simple tricks to help you eat less. First, try eating off of a smaller plate or bowl as a way to trick yourself into thinking you are eating a bigger portion of a meal. One cup of food on a small plate looks like more, than a cup of food on a larger plate. Also try serving food from the stove putting it onto your dishes, rather than placing all the food on the table encouraging seconds. Another trick you could try is when you have the first signs of feeling full, stop eating. There will be more food when you go for your snack or next meal.

6. Meet your fluid and fiber needs

Load up on plenty of fluids. Constipation is a common problem for women post-delivery. To avoid this, try and drink at least eight 8-ounce glasses of water a day, especially if youâ€™re nursing. You can also opt for up to 8 ounces a day of 100% fruit juices, and fat-free or lowfat (1%) milk, sugar-free soft drinks (decaffeinated is preferred) can be included as part of your fluid intake but are nutrient-deficient.

You should also load up on your fiber post-delivery. A good fiber choice is fruit, veggies and whole grains. If you are still having problems with constipation post-delivery try drinking warm liquids or go back to the old remedies of prunes and prune juice. If youâ€™re breastfeeding, try and stay away from high-fiber food that cause you to get gas because it can also make your baby gassy. Beware of cabbage, brussels sprouts, broccoli and beans.

7. Avoid fad diets

Fad diets seem glamorous and like an easy fix for weight problems. The issue that typically follows with these type of diets, are in the beginning you cut calories so drastically that when you resume your prior eating habits, all the weight comes right back. Some new diets also put restrictions on healthy carbohydrates (such as whole grains and fruits). This is a huge mistake. Your body needs these carbs for energy, especially, since they are the main source of energy for your brain. Also these carbs are usually rich in fiber and keep you feeling full. If you plan on cutting out healthy, complex carbs, you should also plan for your body to go into full blown exhaustion. It is advisable to cut out carbs from white pasta, white bread, and rice.

8. Don't be so hard on yourself

Remember that it takes nine months to put on the weight, and you will not lose it overnight or after delivery. It normally takes a year to lose the weight you put on during your pregnancy. Think of it as an 18-month experience: nine months of gestation, nine month postpartum. You are adjusting to your new life of having a newborn baby, possibly breastfeeding, changing your whole lifestyle to fit the baby, and getting your body back in shape. Try not beat yourself up or get frustrated if you donâ€™t snap back as quickly as youâ€™d like too.

Sleep Tips

Sleep Tips for New Moms

Say yes to a helping hand

If you have family or friends that are willing to help with the baby try and be open to it. Making sure you get rest is a necessity for you and your baby, so you stay healthy and have energy. If need be, look for part-time help from a professional.

Snooze with your baby

It may be hard at first, but you should adjust your sleeping time to you babyâ€™s. Having those short naps of 15 to 20 minutes will end up making a big difference. Co-sleeping can also help your baby develop a healthy sleep attitude, because the baby grows up thinking of sleep as a pleasant state to enter and a fearless state to stay in.

Be prepared for the night

A way to make your night go more smoothly with your new baby is to prepare for nightly feedings before hand. Keep everything that you need next to the bed, so that when baby wakes up in the middle of the night it saves you time and effort. Have bottles with already measured out formula and separate pre filled containers of purified water. If you are breastfeeding, then you already are avoiding the hassles of preparing formula bottles in the middle of the night. Another tip is to keep your diaper changing station ready with fresh diapers and baby wipes.

Take turns with your partner

If you are not breastfeeding, then it may be helpful to take turns with your partner to nurse the baby during the night. If you are breastfeeding then you may want to use a breast pump in order to have some pre filled bottles ready, this way your partner will still be able to pitch in. Also try to figure out during pregnancy how you and your partner will deal with baby waking up during the night

Turn down the noise

As a mother, youâ€™re always wanting to know and hear everything that is going on around your child, one of the most common mistakes that new moms make is having the monitor entirely too loud. Your baby monitor should be loud enough to pick up any cries, but it should not be so loud that it disrupts you or your partners sleep. Monitors that have a camera are very useful because if you hear a gurgle you can easily look to make sure that your little one is ok.

Sour Cream combined with grapefruit makes a refreshing antiseptic and astringent base for a homemade facial mask. Its a perfect mask if your skin feels oily and tired.

Strawberries

Strawberries are rich in alpha-hydroxy acids which help exfoliate the skin. So fresh strawberry masks are great for people with dull, damaged or acne-prone skin.

Ingredients: 1/2 tsp lemon juice 1 egg white 1.5 tsp honey 1/2 cup strawberries

Do this:Mash and mix the lemon juice, egg white, honey and strawberries. Apply to your face and leave it on for at least 10 minutes. Rinse off.

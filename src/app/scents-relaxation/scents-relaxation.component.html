Do it Yourself Scents
for Relaxation

All through school I was fascinated at the work that was
accumulated mapping the human gene system. Back then it
was in its infancy but as an adult I paid attention when it was finally
cracked. I had a good idea like many other biochemists and
neurophysiologists, etc. where the main number of genes were attached to.
I had my top ten areas as everyone did...led by Immune Function first of course.
Well my top 10 was intact (no shock) as were others with ONE HUGE EXCEPTION.
The second largest number of genes in the human body belong to the sense of SMELL!! What? When that was announced, it was met with shock and disbelief and then suddenly a great many biochemists began changing their orientation to chase olfactory research. Personally, I was floored. I immediately grabbed my nebulizers and headed to the horse barn to experiment. Lo and behold, I found that certain smells could induce a calm relaxed behavior in a horse, other smells could elicit excitement and fidgeting. Still others could elicit changes in hormones and chemicals in the blood stream. Mind you, these were horses not subject to any placebo effects. Based on that research we moved our studies into the human area.

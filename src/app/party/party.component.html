Choosing A Date

There is no correct day to host a party but it’s important understand that a typical party might last 2-4 hours so often it is better to choose a weekday night or a Sunday. It all depends on the demographics of your guest list. The younger the crowd, the more likely they will be to have other plans on weekend nights.

Normally, you want to choose a date 3-4 weeks in advance to give your potential guests plenty of time to plan to attend.

Guest List

There is no ideal party size but you might want to invite a couple dozen people and hope to end up with 10-12 people at the party.

Make a list of your close friends or family that lives nearby. People who you talk with or share things with on a frequent basis will form your core list of invitees. Second, everyone likes to socialize outside the workplace so invite your co-workers, even those whom you haven’t interacted with that much. Finally, invite people from your school, church, volunteer organization or even local neighborhood.

No matter whom you invite, make a point to extend the invitation for them to bring a friend or two. Having people that you don’t know personally involved is great, because it adds to the group of people who are directly connected to you. Some party hosts actually offer a small gift to anyone who brings an uninvited guest with them!

The Invitation Process

We recommend that you send out your invitations 3-4 weeks in advance.

Traditional paper invitations delivered by the Postal Service can be charming; however, most party hosts like to issue e-invites. My suggestion is to check out Evite.com, a free online invitation service with a variety of invitation templates to choose from. You can incorporate a product image, as well as your website in the invitation, so they are very versatile. Guests can RSVP to Evite.com and the site even sends automatic reminders to guests before the event.

Personally, I would recommend that you give your guests a phone call or a text message a couple days prior to the party. Courtesy communications are always welcome and it gives you the chance to reinforce the fact that new guest are welcome.

Make sure that you mention that the purpose of the party is to socialize. Many people are less threatened by home parties if they do not feel pressured to buy. Everyone loves to “be crafty” so ultimately the jewelry will sell itself. Enjoy your friends, hang out and have a fun evening of friendship.
